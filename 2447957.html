Defining Visual Style: It’s crucial to establish the game’s visual style early on. Is it realistic, cartoonish, or pixelated? The visual style should align with the game’s tone and target audience. As a designer, I’ll help define the look and feel of the game world and how assets should fit together within that context.

For a platformer: I might focus on how the character's animation feels fluid and how the environment is designed with clear cues. For an RPG: I might focus on creating distinct and visually varied characters, creatures, and loot assets.

Characters and NPCs: The player character and NPCs should not only look distinct but also reflect their role in the game (e.g., a hero’s design might be bold and dynamic, while a villager might be simpler). Enemies: Enemies are designed with gameplay mechanics in mind. For example, a flying enemy in a platformer might need to have simple looping animations for its flight patterns.

Week 4

Blocking Out the Level: Making the blueprint of level to outline major structures. Define pathways, platforms, and essential elements without focusing on details.

Week 5

Asset Selection: Choose appropriate assets that fit the theme and style of your level. Optimize assets to maintain performance while ensuring visual fidelity.

Placing Assets: Use modular assets to build the environment efficiently. Snap assets to the grid for precise alignment, but allow slight variations for a natural look.

Week 6

Lighting & Mood Setting: This week I focused on the getting the lighting right for the level 01. Since the level is set in a gladiator style arena, I wanted to create that intense, spotlight driven atmosphere. I played around with directional lights to stimulate those big stadium, then added point and spotlights in specific areas like the ramps, to reeally bring the scene to life. I also tested out of warm tones to help guide player focus and set the mood.

Camera Setup: I started working on a dynamic camera system tailored for car battles. It needed to feel smooth during normal driving but also adapt during high speed stunt and tight combat. I experimented with a few different follow behaviours and camera angle to find a balance that felt cinamatic without making the player lose control or get disoriented.
