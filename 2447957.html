Defining Visual Style: It’s crucial to establish the game’s visual style early on. Is it realistic, cartoonish, or pixelated? The visual style should align with the game’s tone and target audience. As a designer, I’ll help define the look and feel of the game world and how assets should fit together within that context.

For a platformer: I might focus on how the character's animation feels fluid and how the environment is designed with clear cues. For an RPG: I might focus on creating distinct and visually varied characters, creatures, and loot assets.

Characters and NPCs: The player character and NPCs should not only look distinct but also reflect their role in the game (e.g., a hero’s design might be bold and dynamic, while a villager might be simpler). Enemies: Enemies are designed with gameplay mechanics in mind. For example, a flying enemy in a platformer might need to have simple looping animations for its flight patterns.
